Adobe Residency application

The Illustrated Vol. 1

Hello, my name is Juweek Adolphe and I'm 24 years old. I live in Brooklyn, NY and I'm one of the most curious people I know.

Sports, music, culture, politics; they all fascinate me and bring out a desire to learn as much about them as possible. I'm lucky enough to have a job that allows me to learn about things; I'm a visual journalist for the Guardian. What does that mean? It means I experiment with visuals to better tell stories.

Two storytelling methods I've been using have been illustrations and code. I love to draw, and I love to code, and recently I've been experimenting with the two. The more I experiment, the more possibilities I see of using both analog and digital tools to create work. And the more I want to push the limit of what we think of as visual.

I want to make a visual magazine. Over 75% of the world's population are visual learners, and I think in this new world of technology, we should challenge the common notions of things like storytelling and reading on our phone. With a year to explore my process, I can find new ways to merge media together in exciting ways, and portray things in different lights, showing new perspectives and telling beautiful stories.

There are many directions I can take my work, from interactive to data-driven. Here are two projects I made, one an interactive piece about food, the other a data visualization about Congress. Personally, I have the goal of making my work more experimental, so this residency will help me to accomplish that as well.

The timeline would be focused on releasing 4 online magazine issues, released over the course of a year, and hosted on a website similar to most news sites. There is a growing community between artists and coders, and I believe attending conferences will allow me to connect with others and share my work process.

I'm relatively new to Illustrator, so I'll be sharing my journey of learning new techniques, such as using vector files, and drawing on the iPad. I'll also be doing a daily sketch challenge to improve my illustration skills. All of those sketches will be uploaded to Instagram and Behance, so I will engage and encourage the community to do the same.

Speaking of community, sharing will be a large part of my process. S.T.E.M. and the arts are becoming more and more interrelated, and conferences dedicated to the intersection of code and visuals already exist. I have a hand in both pots, and can do a little part in bringing those communities together, as well as challenging the conventional idea of a creative.

Since I was a young kid, I loved learning about the world through stories. Now I'm a stroyteller full-time, and I want to tell the best stories that I can.
