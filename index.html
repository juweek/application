Adobe Residency

Wassup. My name is Juweek Adolphe and I live in Brooklyn, NY. I'm 24 years old, a big fan of basketball, and the youngest child of two Haitian immigrants.

I'm a data journalist for the Guardian, but I'm kind of a jack of all trades; I draw, I code, and I work with data. My dream career would be using varying disciplines to tell stories for editorial brands, like a Mona Chalabi or Christoph Niemann.

My project proposal is to do just that; tell stories with visuals. I want to create a set of data illustrations, experimenting with animation and interactivity to better convey information. Every few weeks, I will collect data on a topic I'm passionate about and visualize it with experimental digital and analog techniques.

I've been doing this for years. I made two editorial articles using data and code; one about how college costs have risen, the other an interactive data viz on the 2018 Congress. There's another page I helped design and code that explained the mission of a satellite lab.

I believe in this new world of technology and exploration, nothing is more exciting than challenging convention. S.T.E.M. and the arts are becoming more and more interrelated, and the definition of creativity changes every day. With a year to explore my process, I can find new ways to merge media together in exciting ways and continue to challenge the conventional idea of a creative. .

The timeline would be focused on releasing according to a schedule and submitting my visualizations to competitions. There are reports that are expected to be released as well. The first few months will be focused on finding ideas to fill my magazine with and also experimenting with analog and digital materials. By June, I will have a focus on what story I want to tell with this magazine.

I'll be integrating Adobe in my illustration workflow, using it to create vector files, and sketch on Adobe Draw on my iPad. I'll be doing a daily sketch challenge to improve my illustration skills and engage the community. All of those sketches will be uploaded to Instagram and Behance.
