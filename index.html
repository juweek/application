The Illustrated Vol. 1

Hello, my name is Juweek Adolphe and I live in Brooklyn, NY. I'm 24 years old, a big fan of basketball, and the youngest child of two Haitian immigrants.

I'm fascinated by a lot of things; sports, music, politics, culture. I'm a data designer for the Guardian, so I'm really interested in the relationship between visuals and information. I would describe my skillset as being able to use visuals to tell stories.

My project proposal is to make a visual data magazine. I will collect data on stories I'm passionate about, visualize them with design, and bring them to life with code.

Now, I know I sound crazy because a jack of all trades, master of none is a hard sell. But I believe in this new world of technology and exploration, nothing is more exciting than challenging the common notions of things like reading on our phones or looking at an image. With a year to explore my process, I can find new ways to merge media together in exciting ways.

There are many directions I can take my work, from interactive to data-driven. Here are two projects I made, one an interactive piece about food, the other a data visualization about Congress. Personally, I have the goal of making my work more experimental, so this residency will help me to accomplish that as well.

The timeline would be focused on releasing online magazine issues, released over the course of a year. The first few months will be focused on finding ideas to fill my magazine with and also experimenting with analog and digital materials. By June, I will have a focus on what story I want to tell with this magazine.

There is a growing community of coders and visual artists intermingling, so I will be attending conferences and hosting meetups in New York to connect more of these creatives. I will be steadily working on my stories and aim to complete 3 by the end of September.

I'm relatively new to Illustrator, so I'll be sharing my journey of learning new techniques, such as using vector files, and drawing on the iPad. I'll also be doing a daily sketch challenge to improve my illustration skills. All of those sketches will be uploaded to Instagram and Behance, so I will engage and encourage the community to do the same.

Speaking of community, sharing will be a large part of my process. S.T.E.M. and the arts are becoming more and more interrelated, and conferences dedicated to the intersection of code and visuals already exist. I have a hand in both pots, and can do a little part in bringing those communities together, as well as challenging the conventional idea of a creative.

Since I was a young kid, I loved learning about the world through stories. Now I'm a stroyteller full-time, and I want to tell the best stories that I can.
